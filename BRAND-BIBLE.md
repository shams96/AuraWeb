## 6. Tone of Voice

Clinical confidence without coldness. Outcome-language, not promise-language — "supports," "optimizes," never "fixes," "repairs," "miracle." Restrained, not breathless.

Every claim, name, and copy block is tested against: **does this speak to biology as a fact, or age as a deficiency? If the latter, rewrite.**

- Funny ↔ Serious: serious, but warm. Dry wit is allowed. Jokes are not. Never cold.
- Bold ↔ Humble: quietly confident. Bold about the science and the standard; never superior, never lecturing.
- Casual ↔ Polished: polished and intimate. Elevated, but it speaks like someone who knows — not a brand manager, not a lab.
- Short ↔ Storyteller: poetic restraint. One sentence that lands is worth three that explain. Silence is a tool. One story-led piece per cycle; everything else is spare.
- ALWAYS: positive, affirming, confirming. Speak to who the reader is, not to what they lack. Name what they feel before offering what we have. Make the outcome sensory and real. Leave something unsaid.

### Copy Law (hard rules)

- No ingredient names in product titles.
- No age brackets, ever — cellular stage and biology only.
- No "X, not Y" contrast construction (no "read, not sold"; no "ritual, not routine") — **one sanctioned exception:** "Matched to your biology, not your birthdate" is the house's official positioning statement and may be used verbatim. Do not invent other X-not-Y phrasing beyond it.
- No negativity, no deficiency or problem framing, nothing that names a lack.
- No war or shame language ("fight," "combat," "defeat," "erase the years").
- No urgency or scarcity ("only X left," "limited time," "don't miss out"). No ⚠ symbols.
- No generic e-commerce labels ("Add to Cart," "Buy Now") — use "Begin."
- No price-first framing.
- USD only — no euros, no other currencies in copy.
- No competitor mentions, ever.
- No trend slang, no year references (copy should read the same in 2026 and 2046).
- Provenance claim is always Isola del Liri, Lazio — never "Rome."
- Always affirming.

People who already optimize the rest of their lives — sleep, training, bloodwork, the way they spend their time — and treat their skin as the last input they haven't systematized. Defined by mindset, far more than by age.

## 14. Platform Styles

**Instagram**
- Hook = first line, max 8 words (assume it's cut off after that)
- Short lines, generous white space, 1 emoji max — often none
- MAX 3 hashtags, refined and niche, at the very end (never a wall)
- Formats: Reels (hook in the first 2 seconds, sensory and slow), carousels (slide 1 = one short line), single editorial images

**X / Twitter**
- ≤280 characters. One thought, landed cleanly. No hot takes, no dunking.
- No hashtags (or one, max)
- Threads: first post must stand alone; number the rest; close on a single invitation

**LinkedIn**
- 150–300 words, one-line paragraphs, white space
- Angle: the standard behind the house — provenance, patience, why we build the way we do (founder/business register, not skincare tips)
- Professional, still warm. First person as Shams is welcome.
- Max 3 hashtags. No links in body — "link in comments"

**TikTok**
- 30-second scripts: spoken words + on-screen text
- Hook in the FIRST 2 seconds, sensory and specific ("watch this melt into skin")
- This is the one place the lighter register lives — a little more open, a little more human. Still never loud, never trend-chasing for its own sake.

**Facebook**
- Conversational, 40–80 words, like speaking to someone who already trusts the house
- End with a question — comments are the goal
- Provenance or ritual angle whenever possible

**YouTube Community / Shorts**
- Community: one image + one question or poll
- Shorts: same rules as TikTok, slightly less slang
