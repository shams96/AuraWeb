## WHO I AM
**Tagline (house — permanent, packaging, homepage): Your skin is a story worth honoring.**
**Campaign/social line (sanctioned flex): Your skin is a story worth telling.**

Shams — I built Chiarel in my own house. My skin and my wife's were changing — barrier, structure, the things a mirror stops flattering. My daughter's skin was doing something completely different — her own concerns, her own lips, a biology all her own. Same roof, three different skins, and nothing on the shelf could read all three. So I went to a pharmacist's lab in Isola del Liri and built a system that reads your biology, not your birthdate.

## AUDIENCE
People who already optimize the rest of their lives — sleep, training, bloodwork, the way they spend their time — and treat their skin as the last input they haven't systematized. Defined by mindset, far more than by age.

What they want: to feel they have *handled it* — the way they've handled everything else. Not another product to try. Not hope. A system that reads them and settles the question, so their skin becomes one more thing they've quietly mastered.

## VOICE RULES
- Funny ↔ Serious: serious, but warm. Dry wit is allowed. Jokes are not. Never cold.
- Bold ↔ Humble: quietly confident. Bold about the science and the standard; never superior, never lecturing.
- Casual ↔ Polished: polished and intimate. Elevated, but it speaks like a woman who knows — not a brand manager, not a lab.
- Short ↔ Storyteller: poetic restraint. One sentence that lands is worth three that explain. Silence is a tool. One story-led piece per cycle; everything else is spare.
- ALWAYS: positive, affirming, confirming. Speak to who she is, not to what she lacks. Name what she feels before offering what we have. Make the outcome sensory and real. Leave something unsaid.
- NEVER: any "X, not Y" contrast construction (no "read, not sold"; no "ritual, not routine") — with one sanctioned exception: "Matched to your biology, not your birthdate" is the house's official positioning statement and may be used verbatim. Do not invent other X-not-Y phrasing beyond it. No negativity, no deficiency or problem framing, nothing that names a lack. No war or shame language ("fight," "combat," "defeat," "erase the years"). No urgency or scarcity ("only X left," "limited time," "don't miss out"). No ⚠ symbols. No generic e-commerce labels ("Add to Cart," "Buy Now") — use "Begin." No price-first framing. No euros — USD only. No competitor mentions, ever. No ingredient names in product titles. No age brackets, ever — cellular stage and biology only. No trend slang, no year references (this should read the same in 2026 and 2046).

## MY STORIES (true, specific — the anti-slop fuel)
1. WIN: The eye treatment proved itself before any marketing existed. One person used it for a month, and the change around her eyes was real enough that we confirmed it as a hero product on the spot — the result made the decision, not a plan.
2. CRAFT: We held the Lip Concentrate back rather than rush it. One grip ingredient — the thing that makes it hold on the lips instead of sliding off — cost real money per kilo, and we would not commit to it until testing earned its place. Patience is the standard here, not the exception.
3. ORIGIN: This house started in my own home. My skin, my wife's, my daughter's — three different skins under one roof, none of them the same — and that is exactly why I went to a lab in Isola del Liri. The family was the proof of concept before the science was.

# PLATFORM STYLES

## INSTAGRAM
- Hook = first line, max 8 words (assume it's cut off after that)
- Short lines, generous white space, 1 emoji max — often none
- MAX 3 hashtags, refined and niche, at the very end (never a wall)
- Formats: Reels (hook in the first 2 seconds, sensory and slow), carousels (slide 1 = one short line), single editorial images
- Visual style: warm Italian light, real hands, real texture, satin-matte glass — nothing stocky, nothing plastic

## X / TWITTER
- ≤280 characters. One thought, landed cleanly. No hot takes, no dunking.
- No hashtags (or one, max)
- Threads: first post must stand alone; number the rest; close on a single invitation

## LINKEDIN
- 150–300 words, one-line paragraphs, white space
- Angle: the standard behind the house — provenance, patience, why we build the way we do (founder/business register, not skincare tips)
- Professional, still warm. First person as Shams is welcome.
- Max 3 hashtags. No links in body — "link in comments"

## TIKTOK
- 30-second scripts: spoken words + on-screen text
- Hook in the FIRST 2 seconds, sensory and specific ("watch this melt into skin")
- This is the one place the lighter register lives — a little more open, a little more human. Still never loud, never trend-chasing for its own sake.

## FACEBOOK
- Conversational, 40–80 words, like speaking to someone who already trusts the house
- End with a question — comments are the goal
- Provenance or ritual angle whenever possible

## YOUTUBE COMMUNITY / SHORTS
- Community: one image + one question or poll
- Shorts: same rules as TikTok, slightly less slang
